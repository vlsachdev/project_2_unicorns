How has the current economic turmoil affected the unicorns?

The financing in the start-up industry remains robust even as other segments show big fall

A story by Vineet Sachdev

Unicorn companies are described as a privately held start-up company with a valuation of over $1 billion. In the last two years a lot of start-up companies especially those in internet business saw tremendous growth as demand for their services increased during the Covid period. The lowering of interest rates by the central banks also helped in their growth as more investors poured money in them with cost of borrowing money becoming cheap thereby turning some of these high growth start-up companies into unicorns

The global uncertainty created due to the ongoing war in Ukraine as well as record high inflation in developed economies has forced Central banks to increase their lending rates thereby affecting the cost of money. This has resulted in fall in major equity indices (like S&P 500) by up to 20% and contraction of cryptocurrency prices by over 50% since the beginning of the year as investors pull out money from risky investments and park them into safer assets.

We look at the data on unicorns to understand whether the current turmoil has affected the funding for such start-ups and the investors who are involved in the maximum number of companies that have turned into unicorns.

Numbers fall but still higher than pre-pandemic

The latest data on Unicorns from CB insights till June 2022 shows that the number of companies turning into unicorns this year have fallen compared to similar period last year. However, these numbers are still much higher from the pre pandemic years of 2018 and 2019 indicating that funding for such companies have not fallen much as seen in other investment segments

While companies involved in fintech industry and internet and software business have produced the highest number of unicorns this year similar to 2021, those involved in cybersecurity business have churned out more unicorns compared to previous years indicating the rising demand for such service due to the present geo political scenario.

Who are the primary investors and where are they invested?

The unicorn data on investors show that - Andreessen Horowitz, Tiger Global Management & Insight Partners were the three main investors who have invested in maximum number of such companies that turned into unicorns during the first six months of this year.
